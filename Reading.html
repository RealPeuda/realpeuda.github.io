Japanese Reading list

In July of 2023 I started learning Japanese. The goal was to be able to play games, watch anime, read manga and books... all in Japanese. Well, it's a been a little over a year and I can say that I can kinda do that stuff now. Kinda. Some works are still a little too intimidating to tackle. So I made a sort of list/reading order to get me up to snuff. I think that if I read everything in the order presented, and keep learning using textbooks and anki, I can pass N2 next summer. So that's the plan!

The Scoring

I gave each entry a difficulty score. These are made with a combination of JLPT levels (N5-N1, N5 being lowest, N1 being highest), elo scores on Learnnatively, and my own personal experiences/assumptions. Here are the different scores and what they mean:


   N5/N4:
Baby stuff. Absolute beginners can understand this.
   N3:
Not particularly hard. Pretty standard language.
   N3+:
Medium difficulty. Similair as N3, but with some extra quirks.
   N2/N2+:
Intermediate. You're getting there.
   N1/N1+:
Nice, you are jouzu.
